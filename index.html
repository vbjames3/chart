NYC Homicides Drop, Fail to Reach Pre Pandemic Levels

by James Van Bramer

November 19, 2022

New York City homicides are expected to drop for the first time since the pandemic began, but will fall short of pre pandemic numbers.

In 2019, New York City had 319, an increase from the previous two years but far fewer than most other years in the 21st century. The NYPD’s most recent data shows the city had 371 murders in 2022 with just over a month left in the year.

In the lead up to the midterms, Republicans bet heavy on crime. The effort fell short on the national level, but showed promise in New York’s Governor’s race. While Kathy Hochul ultimately won, few expected a single digit margin.

Chris Herrmann, a former NYPD crime analysis supervisor, is associate professor of law and police science at John Jay College of Criminal Justice (CUNY) however, was unsurpassed.

“Crime is attractive to voters,” he said. “Especially in New York and especially when it comes to violence.”

Hermmann and his colleague Fritz Umbach argued in a Newsweek op-ed earlier this month that crime hasn’t just risen, it’s changed.

Hermmann said homicides as a whole may be down, but not in all areas. For example, nine people were murdered in New York City subway stations this year, when there were no more than two in the decade before 2018.

Lee Zeldin, the Republican Congressman who nearly defeated Hochul for Governor ran several advertisements directed at safety on the subways.

“These factors make for a winning message,” Hermmann said.
